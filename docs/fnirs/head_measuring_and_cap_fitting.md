# Head Measuring & Cap Fitting

### Overview
Accurate head measurements ensure correct cap placement and data quality. 
Recommend to have a recording sheet.
Remember to ask the participant if they are comfortable throughout the procedure.
---

## Key Landmarks

| Landmark | Location |
|---|---|
| **Nasion (Nz)** | Deepest point between the brow ridge and nose |
| **Inion (Iz)** | Small bump at the base of the skull, back of head |
| **Vertex (Cz)** | Top of skull, midpoint between Nz and Iz |
| **10% Point** | 10% of Nz–Iz distance above Nz |
| **RPA / LPA** | Right / Left preauricular point, in front of the ear |

![head_landmark]({{ '/docs/images/head_landmark.png' | relative_url }})

---

### 1. Take Measurements

1. Measure **Nz → Iz** (mohawk line along midline of skull)
2. Calculate **10% point** (= Nz–Iz ÷ 10), mark on forehead with skin-safe marker
3. Calculate **Nz → Cz** (= Nz–Iz ÷ 2)
4. Measure **RPA → LPA** (headband line across vertex)
5. Measure **head circumference** anchored at brow ridge and inion
6. Record hair color, hair type, and cap size (see Cap Size Chart below)

---

### 2. Select the Correct Cap

**Cap size chart:**

| Circumference (cm) | Cap Size |
|---|---|
| 49.0 – 50.9 | Size 50 |
| 51.0 – 52.9 | Size 52 |
| 53.0 – 54.9 | Size 54 |
| 55.0 – 56.9 | Size 56 |
| 57.0 – 58.9 | Size 58 |
| 59.0 – 60.9 | Size 60 |

> **Note:** If the participant's size falls between two sizes, round up. 
> If the cap causes bumps or raised areas when placed, size down one size.

---

### 3. Place the Cap

1. Ask the participant to tuck their hair behind their ears (if applicable)
2. Be aware of earrings and piercings throughout
3. Place both hands inside the cap and carefully lower it onto the 
   participant's head
4. Align the front of the cap with the **10% mark** on the forehead
5. Pull and adjust until the cap fits

> **Note:** Always place and fit the **parent's cap first** in 
> hyperscanning sessions, to demonstrate the process to the child.

---

### 4. Verify Cap Placement

Confirm the following before proceeding:

- **Fpz** is equidistant between **Nz** and **AFz**
- **Cz** is equidistant between **Iz** and **Nz**
- **Cz** is equidistant between **RPA** and **LPA**
- The **10% mark** on the forehead aligns with the corresponding 
  marker on the cap

> **Note:** If any measurement is off, adjust the cap accordingly 
> before proceeding to optode population.

---

### 5. Next Step

If the cap is already populated with optodes, proceed directly to 
calibration. If not, remove the cap, populate it first, then 
re-fit on the participant.

---

## Cap Fastening Options

| Option | Best For |
|---|---|
| Velcro chin strap (standard) | General use |
| Chin strap (tip of chin) | Protocols involving talking |
| Shoulder harness | Maximum stability |.
